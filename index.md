# Can a better representation abolish search?

I'm **Micheal Ezeoda** (`myketheguru` on GitHub, `_darkmyke` on socials) — a software engineer with
broad scientific curiosity, doing independent research on the foundations of **representation, search,
and computation** — one question at a time,
with engineering rigour: every claim is a small, runnable experiment you can check.

This site is the long-form home of that work. Two public, runnable repos back every claim:
[**representation-dial**](https://github.com/myketheguru/representation-dial) (the foundations + the
active frontier) and
[**concept-rediscovery**](https://github.com/myketheguru/concept-rediscovery) (the original
experiment — including the hypotheses that nulled).

## The series — read in order
1. [Can we abolish search?](/can-we-abolish-search/) — the question, and why search is *conserved*.
2. [Why representation is everything](/why-representation-is-everything/) — the floor, alignment, and the limit.
3. [Computation is explanation](/computation-is-explanation/) — compression can't reach causation; the access/search dial.
4. [How to contribute to the future of search](/how-to-contribute/) — the honest method, and the real frontiers.
5. [The frontier, resolved](/the-frontier-resolved/) — build-in-public: directional → inconclusive → fixed → confirmed.

*The one-line thesis: you can't abolish search, but the representation sets how much of a problem is
cheap geometric access versus irreducible search — and the wins live exactly at the structure, never
beyond it.*
